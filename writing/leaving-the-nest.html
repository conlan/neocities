"Leaving the Nest"

by Conlan Rios | March 2025

Author's Note: This was for the third and final round of NYC Midnight's 250-Word Microfiction Challenge 2024. My genre was open, action was 'slipping', and word was 'harp'. I unfortunately didn't win but am still happy with how it turned out.

It’s breakfast and I struggle to process what’s playing on my son’s iPad. Max, six-years old, shovels down cereal while something angular carves through the air over the fields of Eastern Europe.

“Watching TV?”

The POV shifts to first person and now we’re hurtling down a trench. Max snorts—keeping his eyes on the tablet—as we ram into a terrified soldier, his hands flailing. I catch a Russian arm patch and sickeningly recognize battlefield footage. Max grins at my shock, about to drop some memorized fact.

“The ‘A’ in HARPY stands for au-ton-o-mous,” he says, enunciating each syllable.

* * *

Years later, a HARPY cruising the border mistakenly murders a citizen. My son listens to a podcast while talking heads debate the slippery slope of deploying these weapons so close to home. Leaked footage reveals a diving, metallic raptor overtaking a fleeing figure and Max, opening a soda, mumbles that he watched this clip already.

Apparently, the machine shrieked in Spanish while taking the man apart.

* * *

There’s a tragedy unfolding and I’m glued to my phone. Some maniac has acquired a HARPY and for reasons unknown, unleashed it upon his campus. The livestream shows a bloodied, feathery machine wearing a woman’s face, skittering through a high school hallway. A student stands frozen as the bird-creature screeches, lacerating him with chrome claws.

The room reels as I suddenly recognize my son and the feed cuts out, but not before the thing kicks off into the air, hauling my screaming boy into the pale sky.
